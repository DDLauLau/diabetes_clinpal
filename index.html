DO YOU HAVE DIABETES?

Then you might be interested in joining the Diabetes Clinical Research Study. We want to better understand diabetes and it's treatment and will be using novel data collection procedures to have a more complete view of the treatments, diet and symptoms. This study uses latest patient-centric technologies to make your participation in the trial as convenient as possible and also enables you to be an engaged and informed participant.

Through this website, you can find out more about clinical research studies, find out what the Diabetes Clinical Research Study involves and discover if you can take part. You can even view our live recruitment feed to see how many of you are currently taking part. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact your nearest site.

About the Diabetes Study

In brief

360° view of diabetes

Diabetes is a complex disease and to properly understand the way our current treatments work, it is important to have a more comprehensive view.

This study will capture data about your blood glucose using a wireless blood glucose meter. We will also capture data surrounding times when you may have low blood glucose events (hypoglycemia) in the form of a patient diary that will ask for information about your diet, exercise and insulin use surrounding these events.

The study utilizes a new kind of a clinical trial platform that minimizes the number of site visits during the study and allows almost all of the data capture and study procedures to be conducted remotely. We will send all study materials directly to your home address after you have been enrolled into the study.

Length

You will be on the study for a minimum of 1 month and maximum of 1 year

All patients will visit the study site at least once in the beginning of the trial.

You can learn more about the study before your visit by viewing the study introduction video and you will have an opportunity to ask questions through this Clinpal platform.

Once you are enrolled, you will have full access to all of the features of Clinpal and you will receive step-by-step instructions in each phase of the study.

Readings

These readings will be repeated until 5 correctly measured pairs are completed.

Patients will use a new 3G-enabled glucose meter (which offers wireless connectivity) to conduct personal glucose profiling. The profiling process consists of the following measurements:

  • Evening measurement prior to going to bed
  • Morning measurement in a fasted state
  • Pre-breakfast measurement
  • 2h ± 30 min post-breakfast measurement
  • Pre-main meal measurement (patients can choose their main meal)
  • 2h ± 30 min post-main meal measurement

    About Clinical studies

    What is a clinical research study?

    Clinical research studies are carefully designed scientific studies. Carried out by doctors and nurses, they help us answer important medical questions about investigational medications, such as:

    • How an investigational medication acts in the body
    • How it affects certain diseases or conditions
    • Whether or not it is safe for wider use

    Every year, thousands of people around the world take part in clinical research studies. Independent institutional review boards and ethics committees look after the best interests of these participants:

    • Protecting their rights
    • Ensuring they are not exposed to unnecessary risks
